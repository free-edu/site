Principles

The Free Education Society is committed to the following principles:

  1. Access to knowledge is a fundamental human right. All humans have a right to learn.
  2. Education must be free as in speech.
  3. Education must be practical. We reject the idea of a mandatory "well-rounded" education.
  4. Teachers must teach in a socially-responsible way. Students must use that knowledge in a socially-responsible way.
  5. We teach where people need instruction.
  6. We do not grant degrees, we do not give grades, and we do not pass or fail people. The only person who can determine whether a student is passing or failing is the student themselves.
  7. Anyone who teaches according to these principles may teach as the Free Education Society.
